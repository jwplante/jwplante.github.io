Acadia Visitor Study

Visitor congestion is an issue in Acadia National Park, as it is the most densely visited National Park in the United States. In a team of two, we addressed this problem with an application to track visitor movements throughout the park, which was then sent to a SQL database for further analysis. To assess the viability of this data collection method, we tracked WPI students throughout their hikes in Acadia and determined that it was a viable way to collect data in the park. We generated three recommendations for the park to use.

Main responsibilites included development in Android Studio, writing the back-end server in PHP and creating Python scripts to convert the data into a usable format for Google Earth and ArcGIS.
